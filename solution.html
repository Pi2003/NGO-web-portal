tackling the water crisis one community at a time

Our simple, inexpensive and replicable solutions for clean drinking water and good hygiene are proven to significantly improve the health of entire communities, increase school attendance and empower families to lift themselves out of poverty.

We are trying to find reliable and effective mathods for tackling water crisis. Here's what we're doing today to tackle the water crisis in India and improve the lives of thousands of people.

Clean Water through Solar Disinfection

Solar Disinfection - otherwise known as SODIS - is a simple and sustainable water purification technique that harnesses the sun's UV rays. By filling up a plastic bottle with dirty water and leaving it in direct sunlight for a day (or under cloudy skies for two days), the SODIS method destroys 99.9% of bacteria and pathogens. Simply put, SODIS turns contaminated water into clean drinking water. Because SODIS is the cornerstone of our program, we have improved accessibility to clean water for thousands of people without drilling any new water wells in Africa or building complicated infrastructure.

Concerned about the plastic bottles? So are we. We reuse and recycle as much as possible. 

Rainwater Collection Tanks

Built next to family homes, Rainwater Collection Tanks harness the power of rainy season by collecting up to 6,000 litres of clean, safe water. A simple gutter takes rainwater from the roof directly into the tank. These tanks are specially designed to allow for easy cleaning, and to prevent mosquitoes from breeding in them. The taps are locked to prevent wastage or theft of the valuable water.

Tanks serve 1-5 families who, with clean drinking water at their doorsteps, are less likely to contract waterborne illnesses and have more time to invest in their children, focus on business, attend school and more.

Simple Household Infrastructure

Children can learn to build latrines at home and how to effectively clean up waste in and around their homes and schools. 

This simple infrastructure is essential to the successful implementation healthy sanitation practices.

We have seen that providing communities with sanitation practices can increase school attendance by over 23% because kids aren't out sick or walking for water. 

Habits for Health 

We go back to the basics and provide training in hand-washing, how to clean food before consumption, and more. 

We know that when people have access to clean water and are staying healthy because of their hygiene practices, they have more time and money to invest in small businesses, farming and other activities that drive their economies forward.

 

Composting & Safe Toilets

We work with communities to create garbage and compost pits as well as help build and promote the use of latrines with doors for privacy, and a squat hole cover to prevent flies exiting and contaminating food.

This kind of contamination can also be easily prevented with the use of a latrine with a tight fitting cover - like a SaTo Pan.

The SaTo Pan name is derived from the phrase “Safe Toilet”. SaTo Pans are designed to close off insects and other hosts’ access to faeces - thus limiting their ability to spread diseases.

High Efficiency Cookstoves

We train families on how to build and maintain energy efficient cooking stoves in their homes. These stoves replace open fires that would burn in the middle of a small hut.

High efficient cooking stoves help girls’ and women’s health by drastically improving indoor air quality in the kitchen by venting the smoke outside the hut. In addition they are more environmentally sensitive as they burn about 80% less wood than a traditional open cooking fire.

Menstrual Support

By constructing a private room or outbuilding at school that is stocked with pads, clean underwear, a spare school uniform and basic painkillers, the NGO has found a way to keep more girls in school. When adolescent girls can relieve themselves safely and manage their menstrual cycle privately while at school, they are less likely to drop out.

We've seen that girls in the communities miss 24% of their school days because they don't feel safe coming to school while they have their period. With a few basic supplies and easy access to a safe space, girls feel more comfortable and welcome at school as they grow up.

Empowering Adolescent Mothers to Stay in School

We provide adolescent mothers with the support and advocacy they need to complete their education or vocational training, so that they can secure a better future for themselves, their child and their community.

Adolescent girls often become young mothers before they even have a chance to understand their bodies. Girls receive little to no sex education, which leaves them vulnerable to deceptions or bribery from older men who take advantage of them.

We’re committed to helping these young mothers by connecting them with resources and a support system that empowers them to stay in school.
